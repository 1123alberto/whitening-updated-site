## 3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under GDPR Article 6 and Article 9:

- **Contractual necessity (Art. 6(1)(b)):** to provide dental treatment and manage your appointments.
- **Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)):** to comply with medical record-keeping requirements under Greek law (Law 2071/1992, P.D. 84/2001).
- **Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)):** for website analytics and operational improvements.
- **Explicit consent (Art. 9(2)(a)):** for processing health/medical data and for before-and-after photos used in marketing.
- **Vital interests (Art. 6(1)(d)):** in emergency medical situations.

We use your personal data to:

## 5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our websites use cookies and similar technologies. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

- **Strictly necessary cookies:** required for the website to function correctly. These cannot be disabled.
- **Analytics cookies:** e.g. Google Analytics, to understand how visitors use our sites. Data is anonymised.
- **Marketing cookies:** only with your prior consent, to show relevant advertisements.

You can manage cookie preferences at any time through your browser settings or our cookie consent banner. Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of prior processing.

## 6. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell your personal data. We may share it only with:

- **Dental laboratories:** for the fabrication of dental prosthetics (e.g. crowns, veneers, aligners). Only the minimum necessary data is shared.
- **Referring healthcare providers:** when a clinical referral is required for your treatment.
- **IT and software service providers:** who support our practice management systems, email, and website hosting. These act as data processors under binding agreements.
- **Accounting and legal advisors:** where required by law.
- **Competent authorities:** when required by law (e.g. tax authorities, EOPYY/IKA, judicial orders).

## 8. Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject, you have the following rights:

- **Right of access (Art. 15):** request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
- **Right to rectification (Art. 16):** request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
- **Right to erasure (Art. 17):** request deletion of your data where there is no legal obligation to retain it.
- **Right to restriction (Art. 18):** request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
- **Right to data portability (Art. 20):** receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Right to object (Art. 21):** object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
- **Right to withdraw consent:** at any time, without affecting prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at **info@dentplant.gr**. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

If you believe your rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the **Hellenic Data Protection Authority (HDPA):** [www.dpa.gr](https://www.dpa.gr), 1–3 Kifisias Ave., 115 23 Athens, Tel: +30 210 6475600.
